Shopping in Swedish — At the Shop

Ask for prices, find what you need, and pay with confidence at any Swedish shop or supermarket.

By Lahiru  ·  Expat in Sweden  ·  10 min read
📚 Swedish for Expats — Course Progress
Lesson 5 of 10  ·  Exactly halfway — fantastic progress!
👋 From my experience: Swedish supermarkets like ICA and Coop are straightforward once you know a few words. The self-checkout machines speak Swedish by default. And if you go to a market or bakery, knowing how to ask for the price and say thank you makes the whole experience warmer and easier.

Time to complete: 10–15 minutes  ·  Phrases learned: 14

Key Shopping Phrases

Vad kostar det?
How much does it cost?
Vad kostar det här? — How much is this?
Jag skulle vilja ha…
I would like…
Jag skulle vilja ha ett äpple. — I'd like an apple.
Har ni…?
Do you have…?
Har ni mjölk? — Do you have milk?
Var finns…?
Where is / are…?
Var finns brödet? — Where is the bread?
Jag tar den här.
I'll take this one.
Said when choosing an item.
Kan jag betala med kort?
Can I pay by card?
Sweden is almost cashless — card is always fine.
Kvitto, tack.
Receipt, please.
After paying at the till.
Det är för dyrt.
It is too expensive.
Useful at markets!
💡 Sweden is nearly cashless: Most shops, cafés, and even market stalls take card or Swish (Swedish mobile payment). Don't worry about carrying kronor — but do get a Swedish bank account and Swish app as soon as you can.

A Conversation at the Shop

Lahiru: Ursäkta, var finns brödet?
Excuse me, where is the bread?
Staff: Det är i gång tre, till höger.
It's in aisle three, on the right.
Lahiru: Tack! Och vad kostar det här?
Thank you! And how much does this cost?
Staff: Det kostar tjugo kronor.
It costs twenty crowns.
Lahiru: Jag tar den. Kan jag betala med kort?
I'll take it. Can I pay by card?
Staff: Ja, självklart! Varsågod.
Yes, of course! Here you go.
Lahiru: Tack så mycket!
Thank you very much!
New words: till höger = on the right · självklart = of course · varsågod = here you go / you're welcome · tack så mycket = thank you very much
